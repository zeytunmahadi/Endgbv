About Me - Understanding GBV

Gender-Based Violence (GBV) and Its Effects

Gender-Based Violence (GBV) refers to harmful acts directed at individuals based on their gender. It includes physical, sexual, emotional, and psychological abuse, often resulting in severe and long-lasting effects on survivors.

Trauma from GBV

Survivors of GBV often experience profound trauma, including:

  • Emotional distress such as anxiety, depression, and fear.
  • Physical injuries and health issues.
  • Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) with symptoms like flashbacks and hypervigilance.
  • Social isolation and difficulties in relationships.

How to Help People Who Have Experienced GBV

Supporting survivors of GBV requires a compassionate and comprehensive approach:

  1. Provide Safe Spaces: Create environments where survivors feel safe to share their experiences.
  2. Offer Counseling and Therapy: Connect survivors with professional support to process trauma and develop coping strategies.
  3. Legal and Practical Support: Assist with legal rights, medical care, and practical needs like housing and financial aid.
  4. Educate and Advocate: Raise awareness about GBV, challenge stereotypes, and advocate for policies that protect survivors' rights and prevent violence.
